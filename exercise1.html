Manchester United Football Club was founded in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR Football Club, formed by railway workers. The club changed its name to Manchester United in 1902 after facing financial trouble and receiving new investment.
United won their first league title in 1908 and their first FA Cup in 1909. After World War II, under Sir Matt Busby, the club developed a strong youth policy, leading to the famous “Busby Babes” of the 1950s. Tragedy struck in 1958 when a plane crash in Munich killed eight players.
Busby rebuilt the team and led them to win the European Cup in 1968, the first English club to do so. Under Sir Alex Ferguson (1986–2013),United enjoyed their greatest success, winning 13 Premier League titles, 5 FA Cups, and the 1999 UEFA Champions League, completing a historic treble that year.
Since Ferguson’s retirement, United have had several managers, winning the 2016 FA Cup, 2017 Europa League, and League Cup, while continuing to rebuild to regain past dominance.
